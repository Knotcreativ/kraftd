# Kraftd Market Opportunity: The SME Supply Chain Intelligence Gap

## The Problem You've Identified

### Enterprise vs. SME Supply Chain Intelligence

**Large Enterprises (SABIC, etc.):**
- Structured supply chain data with high-tech endpoints
- Integrated ERP systems with real-time visibility
- APIs and data ecosystems built around them
- Dedicated supply chain teams with analytics capabilities
- Intelligence infrastructure already in place

**SMEs (Raw Material Processors, Exporters, Manufacturers):**
- Perform identical activities: buy materials → process → export → deliver
- **Zero structured supply chain data**
- Fragment manual processes across spreadsheets, emails, documents
- No visibility into pricing trends, supplier risks, market opportunities
- Cannot afford enterprise platforms (SAP, Oracle cost $100K+)
- Forced to meet client compliance requirements (portals, uploads, templates) without getting intelligence in return

### Why Traditional Platforms Fail SMEs

**SAP, Oracle, Vendor Platforms:**
- Designed top-down: Enterprise benefits → Suppliers struggle
- Data architecture favors companies that already have structure
- "Digitalization" is extraction (SMEs input data, enterprises extract value)
- Examples: Construction industry digitalization you experienced
  - Extra hours tailoring raw data to ERP
  - Fancy dashboards kept upgrading
  - Your schedule and effort: **never reduced**
  - Net result: More work, more cost, no efficiency gain

**AI/ML Platforms:**
- Build ecosystems around APIs
- Target enterprises with structured data (SABIC, not SMEs)
- Intelligence is cheap to distribute at scale to enterprises
- Never designed for fragmented SME data
- Never reach SMEs because SMEs lack the infrastructure to integrate

### The Real Cost of "Digitalization" for SMEs

Current digitalization IS NOT efficiency improvement:
- Client portal requirements
- Compliance uploads and templates
- Data entry and formatting to meet templates
- System integrations SMEs can't afford
- **Result:** Increased administrative burden, zero operational improvement

## Why This Market Exists

### The Profitability Paradox
- **Large enterprises:** High CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost), high LTV (Lifetime Value) = Profitable
- **SMEs:** Medium CAC, medium/low LTV = "Not profitable" under traditional SaaS model
- **Result:** No one builds for SMEs; they're abandoned to manual processes

### The Data Problem
- **Enterprise data:** Structured, integrated, clean, accessible via APIs
- **SME data:** Fragmented, unstructured, siloed in emails, documents, spreadsheets
- **Existing platforms:** Built for structured data; ignore SME reality
- **AI/ML requirement:** Train on structured data → fail on unstructured data

### The Gap No One Fills
```
Tier 1: Enterprises (SABIC, etc.)
├─ Structured data
├─ ERP systems
├─ Dedicated teams
└─ Served by: SAP, Oracle, Gartner, etc.

Tier 2: [MASSIVE GAP - UNSERVED]
├─ SMEs with unstructured data
├─ Manual processes
├─ Limited IT resources
├─ Forced to digitalize without tools
└─ Served by: NOBODY

Tier 3: Microenterprises (too small for anything)
└─ Completely manual
```
